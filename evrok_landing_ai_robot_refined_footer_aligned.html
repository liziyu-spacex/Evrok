AI knowledge
Key concepts behind modern artificial intelligence.
This section adds practical AI context to the page so visitors understand what EVROK actually supports — not just the branding, but the technologies powering today’s intelligent applications.
Large language models

LLMs generate and reason with natural language.

Large language models are trained on vast amounts of text to predict and generate language. In products, they power assistants, summarization, search experiences, coding help, and structured decision support.

Retrieval-augmented generation

RAG grounds AI answers in your own data.

Instead of relying only on model memory, RAG retrieves relevant documents at runtime and feeds them into the model. This improves factuality, freshness, and trust for internal knowledge bases and enterprise copilots.

Multimodal AI

Models now understand text, images, audio, and more.

Modern AI can classify images, extract meaning from diagrams, transcribe speech, generate visuals, and combine multiple input types in one workflow — unlocking richer product experiences.

AI agents

Agents chain reasoning with actions.

Agents go beyond single answers. They plan, use tools, retrieve data, execute workflows, verify outcomes, and adapt based on context — making them ideal for automation, support, operations, and research tasks.

Architecture
A practical AI stack, from data to decision.
A modern AI system typically connects private data, retrieval, models, orchestration, and governance. This structure helps visitors immediately understand the foundation behind the product.
Pipeline overview
How EVROK delivers useful outputs
Each layer has a clear role — data provides context, retrieval brings relevance, models generate reasoning, and guardrails keep responses aligned with business rules.
1. Data & context
Structured databases, documents, web content, tickets, product manuals, meeting notes, and internal knowledge sources are indexed and organized.
2. Retrieval layer
Semantic search, keyword search, and reranking retrieve the most relevant evidence before each model call.
3. Model layer
Language, vision, and speech models generate outputs, reason over context, and transform user intent into practical results.
4. Agent layer
Workflow logic can call tools, plan subtasks, make API requests, verify outcomes, and continue execution until the job is complete.
5. Governance
Policies, audit trails, role-based permissions, and safety filters help teams manage risk, compliance, and quality in production.
